Floods near Bern and Luzern

Drag the button left to see flooding along the Aare and Reuss rivers in Switzerland during the July 2021 flood event.

Before — June 2021 (SAR) After — July 2021 (flood detected)
Source: ESA Sentinel-1 · Google Earth Engine · JRC Andrea Salem · switzerland-flooding-gee

Satellite radar imagery shows the extent of flooding across central Switzerland during the July 2021 flood event, when heavy rainfall caused the Aare and Reuss rivers to overflow their banks, affecting communities in the cantons of Bern, Luzern, and Aargau.

An estimated 60.8 km² of land was temporarily inundated — detected by comparing Sentinel-1 radar images taken before (June 2021) and during (12–22 July 2021) the event.

How this was detected SAR (Synthetic Aperture Radar) satellites see through clouds and at night. When land floods, the smooth water surface deflects radar away from the sensor, causing a sharp drop in signal strength. Pixels with a drop greater than 3 dB are classified as flooded. Permanent water bodies (lakes, rivers) are excluded using the JRC Global Surface Water dataset (Pekel et al., 2016).
Uncertainty The ~60.8 km² estimate captures areas of unambiguous water (VV backscatter drop >3 dB) and is conservative by design. Sensitivity to threshold choice: a 2.5 dB threshold yields ~75–80 km²; a 3.5 dB threshold yields ~50–55 km². Partially flooded fields, shallow inundation under vegetation, and radar shadow are likely underrepresented. No field validation; cross-reference with Copernicus EMS EMSR519 recommended.

Data was processed using Google Earth Engine with ESA Copernicus Sentinel-1 GRD imagery (IW mode, VV polarisation, descending orbit). The event is documented in Blöschl et al. (2023), who contextualise it as a 500–800-year return-period rainfall event in the most affected sub-regions.

References: Blöschl et al. (2023) NHESS 23:525–551 · Pekel et al. (2016) Nature 540:418–422 · Gorelick et al. (2017) Remote Sens. Environ. 202:18–27
