In mid-July 2021, record rainfall across central Switzerland caused the Aare river and Brienzersee to overflow, inundating farmland, roads and riverside infrastructure around the town of Brienz (canton of Bern). The event was part of a broader flooding episode that affected communities from Bern to Luzern.

Optical satellite imagery from ESA's Sentinel-2 mission reveals the difference clearly: lush green fields and the defined river channel before the event give way to a pale, sediment-laden floodplain in the aftermath — brown water pooling across the valley floor and depositing fresh alluvial material along the banks.

About the imagery Both images are true-color Sentinel-2 optical composites (bands B4/B3/B2 at 10 m resolution), cloud-masked using the QA60 bitmask (bits 10–11) and median-composited over multi-scene windows — June 10–25 2021 (5 cloud-free scenes; pre-flood peak greenness) and July 18–August 15 2021 (9 scenes; peak flood and immediate aftermath, sediment and altered channels visible). Reflectance scaled ×3.5 for visual clarity. Processed via Google Earth Engine, COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED collection.
Uncertainty Optical compositing cannot penetrate cloud cover; residual cloud shadow may produce dark patches. Mixed-pixel effects occur at 10 m boundaries between water and land. The extent of visible flooding in optical imagery is a visual indicator only — quantitative flood area is best derived from SAR (see Aare & Reuss map, ~60.8 km² detected). The July 2021 event is documented in Blöschl et al. (2023) as a 500–800-year return-period rainfall event in the most affected sub-regions.

The optical view complements the SAR-based flood detection in the Aare & Reuss map: where radar detects standing water through clouds, optical imagery shows the visible landscape change — sediment plumes, debris deposits and inundated fields — in true color.

References: Blöschl et al. (2023) NHESS 23:525–551 · Pekel et al. (2016) Nature 540:418–422 · Gorelick et al. (2017) Remote Sens. Environ. 202:18–27
