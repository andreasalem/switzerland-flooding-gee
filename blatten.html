Glacier collapse buries Blatten village

Drag the button left to reveal the destruction caused by the May 2025 glacier and rock avalanche in the Lötschental valley, Valais. Before: intact village and glacier. After: debris field.

Before — April 2025 After — Summer 2025
Source: ESA Sentinel-2 · Google Earth Engine · Copernicus Andrea Salem · switzerland-flooding-gee

In May 2025, a hanging glacier and large rock mass above the village of Blatten in the Lötschental valley (Canton Valais) collapsed, sending a massive avalanche of ice and rock debris down the valley floor. The village had been evacuated days earlier following warnings from monitoring systems.

Sentinel-2 optical satellite imagery reveals the dramatic transformation: a green Alpine valley with a visible glacier gives way to a vast grey debris field covering the valley bottom. The change is unmistakable even from space.

About the imagery Both images are true-color Sentinel-2 optical composites (bands B4/B3/B2 at 10 m resolution), cloud-masked using the QA60 bitmask and median-composited over multi-week windows to minimise residual cloud and shadow. Reflectance scaled ×3.5 for visual clarity. Processed via Google Earth Engine with the COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED collection.
Uncertainty High-Alpine terrain with persistent snow and cloud cover limits Sentinel-2 availability. Median compositing reduces but does not eliminate cloud shadow artefacts. Optical imagery captures the visible debris field extent but cannot detect material buried under snow or estimate depth/volume. Ground and airborne surveys are required for precise damage assessment. The 2025 Blatten collapse is a rapidly evolving event; imagery may not reflect final extent.

The Lötschental is a high Alpine valley with limited road access. Satellite imagery provided the earliest and clearest documentation of the event extent before ground teams could access the area.

References: Gorelick et al. (2017) Remote Sens. Environ. 202:18–27 · ESA Copernicus Sentinel-2 Mission · Swiss Federal Office for the Environment (FOEN)
